# Teams Pilot Outreach

Last updated: 2026-05-07

## Goal

Start team-pilot conversations that can realistically turn into the first B2B revenue for MicroBreaks.

Primary objective:
- Book pilot conversations with remote and hybrid teams where screen-heavy work is the normal day

Secondary objective:
- Learn which strain pattern is easiest to sell first:
  - eye strain
  - neck and shoulder tension
  - posture fatigue
  - focus drop

## Best early buyers

Target these first:
- 20-200 person remote-first startups
- Product and engineering orgs
- Customer support teams with long screen hours
- Agencies with meeting-heavy and delivery-heavy work
- Distributed teams that already spend on wellness stipends or productivity tools

Avoid first:
- Traditional enterprise procurement-heavy orgs
- Teams asking for SSO, HRIS, or compliance reporting before they care about the product
- Buyers who only want a broad “wellness perk” and cannot describe a specific problem

## Qualification rules

Strong lead if:
- Most of the team works on laptops all day
- The buyer can name real issues like eye strain, tight shoulders, or focus fatigue
- The buyer wants a pilot instead of a perfect enterprise rollout
- The team can test for 2-4 weeks

Weak lead if:
- They ask only about brand, perks, or “employee happiness” language
- They cannot identify a recurring workday strain pattern
- They want a security review before a pilot conversation
- They only want a free trial with no buying intent

## Core pitch

Do not pitch:
- “wellness for everyone”
- “AI productivity coach”
- “habit gamification”

Pitch:
- desk recovery for screen-heavy teams
- guided 2-5 minute resets that fit between work blocks
- a weekly recovery story that shows whether the team is actually using it

## Discovery questions

Ask these in the first call:
- What part of the workday feels physically or mentally heaviest for the team?
- Is the bigger issue eye strain, tight shoulders, posture fatigue, or focus drop?
- Does the team already use any wellness or focus tools?
- Would a 2-4 week pilot be realistic?
- Who would review whether the pilot continues?

## ROI talk track

Use the ROI model only after the buyer names a real strain pattern.

Rules:
- Start with recovered focus minutes, not giant “wellness savings” claims
- Use conservative participation assumptions
- Show the smallest credible commercial shape first
- If the model needs heroic assumptions, do not force the sale

Suggested line:
- “If a portion of the team gets back even a few useful minutes each week, the pilot can justify itself quickly. If that math does not feel believable with your team, we should not force a pilot.”

## Success signal after first call

Strong:
- They can name a real strain pattern
- They accept the pilot-first framing
- They can identify an internal owner
- They ask how a pilot would run

Weak:
- They ask only for pricing and no context
- They want enterprise features before pilot interest
- They cannot identify a user group inside the org

## Intake brief fields

The teams landing now has a structured pilot brief flow.

Capture these fields before the first serious sales call:
- buyer role
- work style
- pilot team size band
- main strain pattern
- timeline
- internal owner clarity
- short workday context note

Use this to decide:
- whether the lead is pilot-ready
- whether the right next step is discovery or pricing
- whether the first offer should stay at `Team Pilot` or move to `Growth Team`

## Pilot close target

Recommended first offer:
- `$499/mo`
- up to 50 seats
- 2 month minimum

Suggested close language:
- “The goal of the pilot is simple: see whether the team actually uses the resets and whether a weekly recovery rhythm starts forming.”
